History

The area that is now Germany was inhabited by Germanic speaking tribes for many centuries. They first became part of the Frankish Empire under the rule of Charlemagne, who is considered the father of the German monarchy. Much of Germany also became part of the Holy Roman Empire. From 1700 to 1918 the Kingdom of Prussia was established in Germany. In 1914 World War I broke out. Germany was on the losing side of the war and is estimated to have lost 2 million soldiers.

In the wake of WWI, Germany tried to recover. There was revolution and the monarchy collapsed. Soon a young leader named Adolf Hitler rose to power. He created the Nazi party which believed in the superiority of the German race. Hitler became dictator and decided to expand the German empire. Foods

German cuisine is hearty, flavorful, and deeply rooted in regional traditions. Each region offers its own specialties, but certain dishes have become beloved across the country. A must-try is the classic Bratwurst, a savory grilled sausage often served with mustard and crusty bread. Another staple is Sauerbraten, a marinated pot roast known for its rich, tangy flavor. You can’t miss Schnitzel either—breaded and fried to golden perfection, it’s a comforting favorite often paired with potatoes or salad. See more...
