History

The area that is now Germany was inhabited by Germanic speaking tribes for many centuries. They first became part of the Frankish Empire under the rule of Charlemagne, who is considered the father of the German monarchy. Much of Germany also became part of the Holy Roman Empire. From 1700 to 1918 the Kingdom of Prussia was established in Germany. In 1914 World War I broke out. Germany was on the losing side of the war and is estimated to have lost 2 million soldiers... German cuisine is hearty, flavorful, and deeply rooted in regional traditions. From sizzling sausages like Bratwurst to crispy Schnitzels and soft, salty Pretzels, the food reflects the country’s rich culture and love for comfort dishes. Famous People

From brilliant scientists like Albert Einstein to legendary composers like Beethoven, some of the world's most influential figures have German roots. Angela Merkel broke political barriers as the country's first female chancellor, and Michael Schumacher made history in Formula One racing. These are just a few examples of how Germany has shaped the worlds of science, politics, music, and beyond. See more...

Culture

German culture is a rich mix of tradition, innovation, and regional customs. It is known for its music, literature, festivals, and engineering. Events like Oktoberfest celebrate food, music, and heritage, while Christmas markets light up cities in winter. Germans value punctuality, order, and quality in everyday life, which can be seen in everything from their public transport to their world-famous cars. See more...
